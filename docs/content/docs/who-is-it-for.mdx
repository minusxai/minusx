MinusX is built for everyone who works with data — but different features matter at different scales. This page helps you find where you fit and what to focus on first.

---

## The MinusX scale

Think of your setup along two dimensions:

- **Data complexity** — how many sources, how much modeling, how much governance you need
- **Team size** — how many people consume or produce analytics

## Solo analyst

**Who you are**: An individual contributor, indie hacker, or early-stage founder who needs quick answers from a database or CSV file.

**What matters most**: Speed. You want to go from question to answer without ceremony.

**Key features to focus on**:
- [Explore](/docs/bi/explore) — ask questions in natural language, get instant results
- [Questions](/docs/bi/questions) — save and revisit your analyses
- [CSV / Google Sheets connections](/docs/data/connections) — upload a file and start querying immediately
- [Visualization](/docs/bi/visualization) — charts with zero configuration

**Start here**: [Getting Started guide →](/guides/getting-started)

---

## Power user

**Who you are**: A data engineer, analytics engineer, or experienced analyst working with multiple databases, complex schemas, and serious SQL.

**What matters most**: Flexibility and depth. You need the tool to handle real-world data complexity without dumbing things down.

**Key features to focus on**:
- [DuckDB / BigQuery / PostgreSQL connections](/docs/data/connections) — connect to your actual warehouse
- [Data Modeling](/docs/data/data-modeling) — create trusted, materialized datasets
- [Pivot tables](/docs/bi/visualization#pivot-tables) — cross-tabulation with formulas and heatmaps
- [Advanced viz settings](/docs/bi/visualization#general-viz-settings) — dual-axis, annotations, column formatting
- [Filters & Parameters](/docs/bi/filters) — dynamic, parameterized queries

**Start here**: [Your First Question →](/guides/first-question)

## Scaling startup

**Who you are**: A growing company where multiple people need access to dashboards and reports, but you don't (yet) have a dedicated data team.

**What matters most**: Self-service. Non-technical team members need to find answers without asking an analyst every time.

**Key features to focus on**:
- [Dashboards](/docs/bi/dashboards) — shared views with interactive filters
- [Text blocks in dashboards](/docs/bi/dashboards#text-blocks) — add context and commentary alongside charts
- [Dashboard parameters](/docs/bi/dashboards#dashboard-parameters) — one filter controls all questions
- [Knowledge Base](/docs/knowledge-base/context) — teach the agent your team's metrics and conventions
- [Folder organization](/docs/bi) — structure dashboards by team or function

**Start here**: [Creating a Dashboard →](/guides/creating-dashboard)

---

## Enterprise data team

**Who you are**: A data team serving dozens or hundreds of stakeholders across the org, with governance requirements, multiple data sources, and a need for reliability.

**What matters most**: Trust, governance, and scalability. Every number needs to be traceable to a trusted dataset.

**Key features to focus on**:
- [Data Modeling](/docs/data/data-modeling) — single source of truth with clear grain and business logic
- [Context hierarchy](/docs/knowledge-base/context#tree-based-whitelisting) — org-wide governance with team-level access control
- [Evals](/docs/knowledge-base/evals) — measure and track AI accuracy systematically
- [Proactive Analytics](/docs/proactive) — scheduled reports and threshold-based alerts
- [Permissions & modes](/docs/installation) — user management and environment isolation

**Start here**: [Knowledge Base Setup →](/guides/knowledge-base-setup)

---

## The bottom line

You don't need to use everything on day one. Start with what matters for your scale, and unlock more as you grow:

1. **Day 1** — Connect a database, ask questions in Explore
2. **Week 1** — Save questions, build your first dashboard
3. **Month 1** — Set up the Knowledge Base, run evals, share with your team
4. **Ongoing** — Add data models, automate with reports and alerts, refine context based on eval results
